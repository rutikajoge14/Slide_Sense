Disaster Preparedness

Preparation Steps:
  • Emergency Kit: Prepare a kit with water, food, first aid, flashlight, radio, and batteries
  • Evacuation Plan: Know your evacuation routes and have a family meeting point
  • Stay Informed: Monitor weather forecasts and register for alert systems
  • Property Protection: Build retaining walls, plant ground cover on slopes
  • Drainage: Ensure proper drainage around your property
  • Insurance: Check if your insurance covers landslide damage
  • Warning Signs: Learn to recognize landslide warning signs

Immediate Actions:
  • Alert Others: Warn neighbors if possible
  • Evacuate: Move away from the path of the landslide immediately
  • Go Uphill: Move to higher ground perpendicular to the slide path
  • Avoid Rivers: Stay away from river valleys during heavy rainfall
  • If Indoors: Curl into a tight ball and protect your head
  • Stay Alert: Watch for flooding which often accompanies landslides
  • Listen: Unusual sounds might indicate moving debris

Recovery Steps:
  • Stay Away: Keep away from the slide area as it may slide again
  • Check for Injuries: Provide first aid if trained
  • Report Hazards: Report broken utilities to authorities
  • Replant: Plant ground cover to protect slopes from erosion
  • Seek Advice: Consult geotechnical expert before returning home
  • Watch for Changes: Monitor for new cracks, tilting, or ground movement
  • Document Damage: Take photos for insurance claims

Recognize These Signs:
  • Changes in landscape patterns
  • Springs, seeps, or saturated ground in areas that are typically dry
  • New cracks in plaster, tile, brick, or foundations
  • Sunken or down-dropped road beds
  • Tilting trees, telephone poles, or fences
  • Broken water lines or underground utilities
  • Bulging ground at the base of slopes
  • Water breaking through ground surface
  • Unusual sounds like trees cracking or boulders knocking together
